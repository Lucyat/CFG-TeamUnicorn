Singapore

With its century-old temples, bustling hawker centres and lush green spaces, Singapore’s varied charms are bound to enchant visitors to our island. The city’s calendar of events is equally diverse, and present travellers with ample opportunities to explore, indulge and express their passionss.

Singapore’s unique hawker food culture is equally impressive, with the hawker centres serving up dishes that span Chinese, Malay and Indian culinary traditions from chicken rice, fish head curry to rojak and satay.
Singapore weather is always warm, with temperatures averaging around 32°C during the day and 25°C at night from December to January while the rest of the year averages around 33°C during the day and 26°C at night. The locals themselves dislike the heat so you'll find that everywhere you go is air conditioned.
Singapore is known for its impeccable cleanliness and low crime rate. The country has such a strong reputation for being safe that the authorities had to put out a warning stating that “low crime does not mean no crime,” reminding people to stay vigilant.
